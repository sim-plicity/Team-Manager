# Profiles Management Guide

Profiles let you save your favorite wheelbase settings and switch between them instantly. Instead of manually adjusting all the sliders every time you want to switch from GT racing to drifting, you can save each configuration as a profile and load it with one click.

## Profiles Tab Overview

The Profiles tab is straightforward:

**What You'll See:**
- **Profile dropdown**: List of all your saved profiles
- **Preview window**: Shows what settings are in the selected profile
- **Action buttons**: Save, Load, New, Rename, Delete

**Key Idea:** Profiles store ALL your settings - both basic and advanced - so you can switch between completely different wheelbase setups instantly.

## Creating Your First Profile

### Step 1: Set Up Your Wheelbase
1. **Adjust all your settings** in the Configuration tab
2. **Test the settings** in your favorite game
3. **Fine-tune until you're happy** with how it feels

### Step 2: Save Your Profile
1. **Go to the Profiles tab**
2. **Click "New Profile"**
3. **Give it a descriptive name** like:
   - "GT Racing - Daily Driver"
   - "Drifting - Quick Response"
   - "Formula Cars - Precision"
   - "Beginner - Gentle Feel"
4. **Click "Save Profile"**

**That's it!** Your settings are now saved as a profile.

## Loading Profiles

### Quick and Easy
1. **Select your profile** from the dropdown list
2. **Click "Load Profile"**
3. **Click "Apply"** in the Configuration tab
4. **Start driving** - your wheelbase now uses the new settings

### Preview Before Loading
The preview window shows exactly what settings are in each profile. This is helpful because:
- **No surprises** - you can see what torque, rotation, and other settings you'll get
- **Easy comparison** - select different profiles to see the differences
- **Quick reminders** - refresh your memory on what each profile is for

## Managing Your Profiles

### Renaming Profiles
1. **Select the profile** you want to rename
2. **Click "Rename Profile"**
3. **Type the new name** and press Enter

### Deleting Profiles
1. **Select the profile** to delete
2. **Click "Delete Profile"**
3. **Confirm the deletion**

**Be careful:** Deleted profiles cannot be recovered.

### Creating Variations
Once you have a profile you like, you can easily create variations:
1. **Load your base profile**
2. **Make small adjustments** (like increasing torque for racing)
3. **Save as a new profile** with a name like "GT Racing - Race Day"

## Practical Profile Examples

### Everyday Profiles Most Users Need

**Daily Driver:**
- **Rotation**: 900°
- **Torque**: 60-70%
- **Use for**: General racing, most games
- **Name suggestion**: "Daily Setup" or "General Racing"

**Precision Setup:**
- **Rotation**: 540-720°
- **Torque**: Slightly higher
- **Use for**: Competitive racing, time trials
- **Name suggestion**: "Race Setup" or "Precision"

**Beginner Friendly:**
- **Rotation**: 900°
- **Torque**: 30-40%
- **Use for**: Learning new tracks, casual driving
- **Name suggestion**: "Learning" or "Gentle"

**Game-Specific Profiles:**
- **iRacing Setup**: Optimized for iRacing
- **Assetto Corsa**: Settings that work well with AC's FFB
- **Project Cars**: Configured for PCars physics

### How Many Profiles Do You Need?
**Most users are happy with 3-5 profiles:**
- One general-purpose setup
- One for competitive racing
- One for learning or casual driving
- Maybe one or two game-specific setups

Don't overcomplicate it - start simple and add more profiles as you identify specific needs.

## Profile Best Practices

### Naming Tips
Good profile names help you quickly find what you need:
- **Be descriptive**: "GT Racing - Medium Torque"
- **Include the purpose**: "Practice Setup", "Race Day"
- **Keep it consistent**: Use the same format for all names

### When to Create New Profiles
- **Different car types**: Formula vs GT vs Rally
- **Different goals**: Practice vs Racing vs Casual
- **Different games**: Some sims feel better with different settings
- **Major preference changes**: When you significantly adjust your feel

### Profile Organization
**Start simple:**
- Create profiles as you need them
- Delete ones you don't use
- Rename things that aren't clear
- Don't worry about perfect organization

**Evolve over time:**
- You'll discover what works for you
- Your preferences might change as you improve
- Different games might need different approaches
- Your setup might evolve with experience

## Quick Reference

### Common Profile Workflow
1. **Adjust settings** until they feel right
2. **Save as profile** with clear name
3. **Test in game** to confirm everything works
4. **Tweak if needed** and save again
5. **Create variations** for different uses

### Troubleshooting Quick Fixes
**Profile won't load:**
- Try restarting the application
- Make sure wheelbase is connected
- Load a different profile to test

**Settings seem wrong:**
- Check the preview window to see what's actually saved
- Remember to click "Apply" after loading
- Try loading the profile again

**Too many profiles:**
- Delete ones you don't use
- Combine similar profiles
- Rename things to be clearer

### Most Important Tip
**Don't overthink it!** Profiles are meant to make your life easier. Start simple, add more as you need them, and don't worry about perfect organization. The goal is to spend more time driving and less time adjusting settings.
